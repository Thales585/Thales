## Kill criteria as they will be executed (no amendment mid-run)

- **K1 Viability:** market cap ≥ ~$500M and no going-concern language. The farmland
  REITs are the candidates at risk here.
- **K2 Seat authenticity:** DE dies as engine if ag equipment is a minority of revenue
  or the stock demonstrably trades on construction/macro. ADM/BG die as tollbooth if
  their economics are commodity-trading P&L rather than volume-fee collection. A
  farmland REIT dies if rents are not actually collected independent of crop outcomes
  (crop-share leases > ~50% of revenue would fire this).
- **K3 Window survival:** positive trailing-12M total return AND anchor max drawdown
  better than −60%. Executed on search-verified anchors only.
- **K4 Self-funding:** any forced dilutive raise at depressed prices in the trailing
  window kills. ADM's accounting scar escalates scrutiny but is not itself a kill.
- **K5 Non-redundancy:** pairwise monthly-return correlation < ~0.9 vs an occupied
  seat. Expected tangle: ADM vs BG (same business) — at most one survives seat
  contention; DE vs CTVA compete for engine, loser is discarded not killed.

## Scenario inversion claim (pre-registered)

Bumper-crop glut → grain prices fall → engine (equipment/seed pricing power) starves,
farmland rents lag but persist (tollbooth), protein margins expand on cheap feed
(inverse feeds). Weekly price decorrelation is NOT claimed — scenario level only.

## Uninsured quadrant (named in advance)

A demand-side food recession with high input costs: engine starves (no farmer capex),
farmland cap-rates widen (tollbooth marks down), protein demand and margins compress
(inverse starves). All three lose together. Also: a rates shock hits FPI/LAND hardest.
