## The finding, stated plainly

**The space economy is eating its own tollbooths.** Every K3 survivor with toll-like
economics is being vertically integrated or absorbed before a public investor can hold
the toll: Rocket Lab (the engine) is acquiring Iridium (the toll); Amazon is acquiring
Globalstar's satellite operations, taking the Apple-contracted fees inside Amazon Leo
(SEC 8-K, Apr 2026); EchoStar's story is spectrum monetization, not toll collection.
Meanwhile the pre-registered inverse (ViaSat) quintupled on Space Force prime awards, a
$4.82B NASA contract, and spectrum re-rating — a *boom* narrative that empties its
LEO-stumble inverse claim. After three inner-loop iterations, two of three seats are
unfillable. Per protocol, the honest output is **no valid trio** — a structural result
about the sector, Water-class, not a failure of the run.

## What would reopen the sector

A public, standalone toll: launch insurance pure-play, ground-station/relay operator
IPO, or a post-merger RKLB whose Iridium service revenue can be tested as internal
tollbooth economics (that is a *different* thesis — engine-with-annuity — and would
need its own gauntlet). Also a genuine inverse would need a name that *feeds* on
space-capex disappointment; none exists on Alpaca today.
