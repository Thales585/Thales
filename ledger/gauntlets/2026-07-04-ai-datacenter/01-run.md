## The finding, stated plainly

**Nothing tradable feeds on an AI-capex bust.** The scout predicted it; three
pre-named inverse attempts died on schedule. In this regime, everything adjacent to
datacenter power is either positively exposed to the boom (utilities grow rate base —
SRE's 0.49 correlation to VRT is the confession), or trades on an entirely different
war (CENX on tariffs, aluminum, and the Gulf — power costs a footnote), or lives in a
different regime altogether (MARA and bitcoin). The inverse seat for this sector does
not currently exist on a public US exchange. Engine and tollbooth are clean and
seatless — a two-legged stool the book refuses to sit on.

## What would reopen the sector

An instrument that genuinely monetizes the bust: distressed-datacenter vehicles,
a power-arbitrage pure-play, or post-bust vacancy REIT structures — none public today.
Alternatively, accept a two-seat construction under different rules — which is a
different method, and would require amending the skill *between* runs, in writing.

## Record

Seatless duo VRT/EQIX 57/43 backtested +73.8% / −13.3% (naive VRT +106.6% / −16.0%) —
recorded so the cost of discipline is visible at the horizon grade. If the boom runs
another year, this finding will look expensive; if it busts, the missing seat was the
point. Both outcomes go in the calibration ledger.
