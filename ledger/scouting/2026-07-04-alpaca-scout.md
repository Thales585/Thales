# Alpaca universe scout — candidate sectors for gauntlet runs

- **Date:** 2026-07-04
- **Scope:** pre-gauntlet scouting only. Nothing below is a position or a survivor —
  every name still enters the gauntlet and may be killed. No prices are cited because
  none were searched; K3 (window survival) and K4 (self-funding) verdicts belong to the
  run, not the scout. What *was* verified today: every ticker below is tradable and
  fractionable on Alpaca (assets API, 2026-07-04), except where flagged.
- **Existing book (excluded):** Uranium, Electrons, Metals, Procurement. Logged
  findings: Water (inverse seat unfillable), Shipping (killed on timing).

The scan looked for sectors where all three seats — engine, tollbooth, regime-inverse —
have a *structurally plausible* Alpaca-listed occupant, proxies acceptable. Six sectors
were examined; four present clean slates, one carries a known weak seat, one is
flagged as a likely finding.

---

## 1. Agriculture — cleanest slate, recommended first run

The scenario inversion is the attraction: the thing that kills the engine (a bumper-crop
glut crushing grain prices) is the thing that feeds the inverse (protein and packaged
food margins expand on cheap feed). That is a genuine mechanism, not a correlation hope.

| Seat | Candidates (Alpaca-verified) | Seat logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine | DE (Deere), CTVA (Corteva) | Precision-ag / seed-science growth; dense earnings calendar — dated catalysts to verify at run time |
| Tollbooth | FPI or LAND (farmland REITs); ADM / BG (processing-trade flows) | Farmland rent collects regardless of which crop or farmer wins — presses, not pickaxes. ADM/BG collect on volume, but K2 must test whether trading desks break seat purity |
| Regime-inverse | TSN (Tyson) | Cheap-feed beneficiary; its best regime (grain glut) is the engine's worst |

**Known risks to pre-register:** FPI/LAND are small caps — K1 liquidity check required.
ADM carries an accounting-investigation scar — K2/K4 scrutiny. Deere is a conglomerate
of ag + construction — K2 authenticity test.

## 2. Housing regime — clean inversion, macro-heavy

High-rates-for-longer starves the builders and feeds the single-family-rental REITs that
collect rent from the priced-out. The inversion lives on one macro axis (rates), which
is both its elegance and its concentration risk — the uninsured quadrant is probably
"rates high AND rents soft."

| Seat | Candidates | Seat logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine | DHI, LEN | Volume builders; monthly starts/permits + quarterly earnings = dated catalysts |
| Tollbooth | BLDR | Supplies every builder regardless of which wins share. K2 test: is a distributor a tollbooth or a pickaxe? Must argue at run time |
| Regime-inverse | INVH, AMH | Rent capture from the priced-out; best regime = builder's worst |

## 3. Defense / drones — needs the proxy, inversion is cross-sector

Rheinmetall (the obvious European engine) is **not tradable** on Alpaca (RNMBY is OTC,
halted). EUAD (Select STOXX Europe Aerospace & Defense ETF) is the tradable proxy —
same K2 dilution caveat as LIT-for-CATL in the live Electrons trio, logged from day one.

| Seat | Candidates | Seat logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine | KTOS / AVAV (drones, dated program awards); EUAD as Europe-rearmament proxy | Program wins and delivery milestones are dated catalysts |
| Tollbooth | TDG, HEI | Proprietary aero parts collect on every flight hour, military or civil — royalty-like economics |
| Regime-inverse | DAL (or peer airline) | The peace dividend: cheap fuel, open skies, travel boom — defense's worst regime |

**Pre-registered concern:** the inverse is cross-sector (an airline is not "the defense
sector"), so K2 must be argued at scenario level; and TDG/HEI collect on *civil* aero
too, which softens their defense-seat purity. Both arguable — at run time.

## 4. GLP-1 aftermath — strong tollbooth, engine has a scar

| Seat | Candidates | Seat logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine | NVO, LLY | Obesity-drug franchise; readouts and supply milestones are dated. NVO's trailing-year drawdown may fire K3 on contact — verify, don't assume |
| Tollbooth | MCK, COR | Drug distribution collects per unit moved, whichever molecule wins — near-perfect seat fit |
| Regime-inverse | DVA (dialysis) | If GLP-1 renal benefits disappoint, the chronic-kidney caseload persists — its best regime is the engine's failure |

**Note:** the tollbooth here (MCK/COR) may be the single cleanest seat in the entire
scout.

## 5. Space economy — engine-rich, tollbooth-poor (weak seat flagged)

RKLB and ASTS give a dense dated-catalyst engine (launch manifests, constellation
deployments). IRDM is the tollbooth candidate (connectivity fees regardless of which
rocket flies), but it competes with the engines' own constellations — possible K5/K2
tangle. No obvious third seat: what feeds when space disappoints? Terrestrial telecom
is too diffuse. **Inner-loop widening required before this sector earns a run.**

## 6. AI-datacenter power — likely a finding, logged honestly

Engine (VRT) and tollbooth (EQIX/DLR — rent on every rack, whichever model wins) are
clean. The regime-inverse seat may be **unfillable**: nothing Alpaca-listed obviously
*feeds* on an AI-capex bust rather than merely dodging it. SRE-style regulated utilities
dodge; they don't feed. Unless the inner loop finds a genuine occupant, this repeats
the Water finding — an unfillable seat is a result about the sector, not a failure of
the method.
