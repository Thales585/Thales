## Page 1 — Doctrine: events as stop-losses

### Why events, not prices

A conventional stop-loss fires on price. This book fires on **evidence**. The reasoning
is native to the method: every position was entered on a pre-registered mechanism, so
the only honest exit trigger is the observable event that shows the mechanism dying.
Price stops on a 12-month buy-and-hold book amputate positions during noise (the
regime-inverse legs are *designed* to bleed in the base case) — but events do not
mean-revert. A restatement, a budget reversal, a distressed raise: these are one-way
doors. The stop-loss activators below are therefore **real-world events**, with price
levels retained only as backstops of last resort.

### The three-tier activation ladder

Every trigger in this report carries one of three tiers, and each tier compels an
action — the discipline is that observation *must* produce a logged response:

- **WATCH** — evidence is drifting against the hypothesis. Action: log it, date it,
  tighten the search cadence on that leg. No trade.
- **STRESSED** — a kill-line precondition has occurred once (most kill-lines need two
  consecutive quarters, or confirmation). Action: schedule an early checkpoint for the
  leg; pre-draft the exit note so execution is mechanical if confirmation lands.
- **KILL / EXIT** — the pre-registered kill-line has fired. Action: close the leg at
  next session, log the kill as a conscious act, send the empty seat back through the
  inner loop (replace or dissolve the trio). *No averaging down, no goalpost moves —
  criteria amend only between runs.*

Price backstops (all legs, uniform): **−30% from fill = WATCH · −45% = STRESSED
(forced early review) · −60% = KILL** (the K3 line, applied forward). These exist so a
mechanism-death we failed to observe still cannot destroy a seat silently.

### Portfolio-level activators

Two triggers sit above the sectors. **(1) Correlation collapse:** if a week arrives
where every trio's three legs move together beyond ±5% in the same direction, that is
the regime speaking, not eight theses — freeze all exits for 72 hours, attribute, then
act (macro P&L events are logged, not graded). **(2) The uninsured quadrants:** each
trio named the scenario that hurts all three legs. If a named quadrant begins
(pricing legislation clearing committee, austerity budgets tabled, demand-side food
recession), the trio's exit review begins *even though no leg-level line has fired* —
quadrants are trio-level stop activators.

### Standing orders for every exit

An exit is a logged, dated act citing the activator, the tier ladder it climbed, and
the attribution (thesis-death vs regime vs noise). The seat goes back through the
inner loop within one checkpoint cycle: replace the leg or dissolve the trio —
a two-legged trio may not stand. P&L at exit is recorded but **not graded**; the grade
belongs to the mechanism (H1–H8 in the [hypothesis register](../ledger/hypotheses.md)).
Green exits on dead mechanisms are rejections that got lucky, and are logged as such.
