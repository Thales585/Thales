*Paper trading only — a calibration exercise for the Thales ledger, not financial
advice. This is a seat map, not a verdict: every name below still enters the gauntlet
and may be killed there.*

**Prior-ledger flag before anything else:** Water is already in the book as a
*finding* — the 2026-07-04 scout logged "Water: inverse seat unfillable." Your
hypothesis is narrower than the one that produced that finding (it names a mechanism —

## Hypothesis (restated, falsifiable)

Federal water appropriations (BIL/IIJA State Revolving Fund tranches and the lead
service line replacement mandate) convert into municipal capex orders within 24 months
(by 2028-07), showing up as backlog and revenue acceleration at municipal-water
pure-plays and rate-base growth at regulated water utilities; **falsified if** federal
disbursements stall, are rescinded, or are absorbed by cost inflation such that
pure-play order books show no acceleration across the horizon's earnings prints.

## Proxy rungs used (if any)

- **Rung 2 — synthetic seat via short: short(TTEK)**, substituting for a missing
  natural feeder on thesis failure. Logged costs, all three, per the ladder:
  1. It is a **hedge, not a feeder** — non-cannibalization dies by construction: if
     the funding lands and the whole sector runs, the short leg loses while the other
     two win; the three seats can no longer all win together.
  2. It is **not the engine itself** (that rule is satisfied — MWA/XYL are the engine
     candidates), but TTEK is still thesis-correlated, so K5 must check that this leg
     is not just "less engine" wearing a services costume. TTEK's non-water federal
     and international book also cushions the short — the failure mode may not
     transmit cleanly.
  3. **Carry and borrow costs decay the seat**, and in live markets the loss is
     unbounded; on the Alpaca paper account shorting is enabled (TTEK verified
     shortable/ETB, 2026-07-05), so it is testable — paper only.
- **Rung 3 remains the standing expectation.** PRMB is a genuine direct candidate, not
  a proxy, but it is a weak one, and the rung-2 short is a hedge by construction. If
  the gauntlet kills both — which the prior Water finding predicts — the honest result
  is again **no-valid-trio**, and the sector stays a finding until something like a
  listed distressed-muni-water vehicle, a water-quality remediation pure-play, or a
  point-of-use filtration pure-play lists on an Alpaca-tradable exchange. A forced
  weak occupant is worse than an honest hole.

## Uninsured quadrant (first guess)

Federal rescission **plus** municipal fiscal squeeze **plus** consumer downtrade: the
engine's backlog stalls (no orders), CNM's flow-toll starves with it, AWK/WTRG face
hostile rate cases from strapped ratepayers, PRMB's premium bottled water gets
down-traded to tap-and-tolerate in a recession, and even short(TTEK) can fail to pay
if TTEK's non-water book cushions the stall. Everything loses at once; nothing in
this slate insures that corner, and the pre-registration must say so.
