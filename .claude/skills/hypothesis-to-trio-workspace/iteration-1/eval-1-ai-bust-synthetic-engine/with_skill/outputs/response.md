## Hypothesis (restated, falsifiable)

The AI-datacenter capex cycle cracks by ~January 2028 (18 months): at least one hyperscaler cuts capex guidance, GPU/server orders are publicly cancelled or deferred, and GPU lead times collapse back toward book-and-ship normality — proven wrong if hyperscaler capex guidance keeps rising and lead times remain extended through the horizon.

Note the mirror-image structure: this is the *bust* of the AI-DC boom thesis. The seat our ledger previously found unfillable (AI-DC's regime-inverse — nothing listed *feeds* on the boom failing) is now the **engine** seat of this hypothesis. The hole doesn't vanish when you flip the thesis; it moves to the front of the trio. That forces the proxy ladder immediately.

## Proxy rungs used

- **Engine, rung 2 (synthetic seat via short): short(SMCI) or short(VRT).** No listed asset's revenue line *grows* when GPU orders get cancelled — cheap-compute beneficiaries (app-layer, secondary-market GPU brokers) are either conglomerates or unlisted. Logged costs, in full:
  1. **Hedge, not feeder** — non-cannibalization dies by construction: in a sector-wide AI bull, engine (short) and inverse (long) cannot both win; the trio can no longer all win together.
  2. **Mirror-redundancy guard** — the engine short and the inverse long must be different names (enforced above), else K5 at correlation −1.
  3. **Carry and decay** — borrow cost bleeds the seat every week the crack doesn't come; in live markets the loss is unbounded. Shorting is enabled on the Alpaca paper account, so it is testable — paper only.
- **Engine, rung 1 alternative: SOXS.** Cost: K2 authenticity erosion — grading must reference semiconductor capex/lead-time reality, never the SOXS price, because 3x daily-reset path dependence can lose money even in a slow grind down over 18 months.
- Tollbooth and inverse seats fill natively — no rungs.

## Uninsured quadrant (first guess)

**The orderly plateau.** Capex decelerates smoothly — guidance flattens rather than cracks, orders are stretched rather than cancelled, nobody files. The engine shorts bleed borrow and decay against names that grind sideways on existing backlog; the tollbooth gets no restructuring-fee wave because there is no distress, only digestion; the inverse longs stall as the growth premium compresses without a boom to feed on. A soft landing starves all three seats at once — this trio needs the cycle to *break*, not merely cool.
